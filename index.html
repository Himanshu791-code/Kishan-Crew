🌿 Recommended Organic Fertilizers

Safe, effective, and improves soil health long-term

▶️
Watch: Making Jeevamrit at Home (6 min)
🐄
Jeevamrit (जीवामृत) Mix 10L cow urine, 10kg cow dung, 2kg jaggery, 2kg gram flour in 200L water. Leave 48 hours. Apply 200L/acre every 15 days.
🍂
Vermicompost (केंचुआ खाद) Apply 2–3 tonnes/acre before sowing. Increases soil organic matter, improves water retention. Best for vegetables & herbs.
🌱
Green Manure (हरी खाद) Grow dhaincha or sunhemp, then plough into soil 45 days later. Adds nitrogen naturally — equal to 80kg urea/acre.
🦠
Bio-fertilizers (जैव उर्वरक) Rhizobium for pulses, Azotobacter for wheat & vegetables. Available free at Bhopal Krishi Vigyan Kendra.

🛡️ Safe Pesticide Guidance

Organic alternatives + safe usage of approved pesticides

🌿
Neem Oil Spray (नीम का तेल) — Best organic option Mix 5ml neem oil + 1ml soap per litre water. Spray in evening. Controls aphids, whitefly, fungal diseases. Safe for humans.
🧄
Garlic-Chilli Spray (लहसुन-मिर्च अर्क) Blend 100g garlic + 10 red chillies + 1L water. Strain and dilute 1:10. Repels insects. Spray every 10 days.
⚠️
If using chemical pesticides — Safety Rules Always wear gloves + mask. Spray in early morning or evening only. Do NOT spray 14 days before harvest. Store locked away from children.
📞
Free Expert Help Available Bhopal Krishi Kendra helpline: 1800-180-1551 (free, Mon–Sat 9am–6pm). Get advice specific to your crop.

💧 Watering Schedule Guide

Optimal irrigation for Bhopal's climate

🍅
Tomatoes Water every 3–4 days (summer) or 6–7 days (winter). 4–5 litres per plant per watering. Avoid waterlogging — causes root rot.
🌾
Wheat (Gehu) 4–6 irrigations total. Critical stages: CRI (21 days), tillering, jointing, flowering, grain filling. Each time 5cm depth.
🧅
Onion Water every 7–8 days. Stop irrigation 10 days before harvest to harden bulbs. Drip irrigation saves 40% water.
🥬
Leafy Vegetables (Palak, Methi) Light watering every 2 days. Avoid overhead spray — causes fungal disease. Use drip or furrow irrigation.
💡
Pro Tip: Water in Morning or Evening Only Midday watering wastes 30–40% to evaporation. Early morning is best — plants absorb better, less disease risk.
